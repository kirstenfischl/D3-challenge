Correlation Discovered Between Poverty and Healthcare Access

In general, states with a higher percentage of their population living in poverty are also have higher levels of their population without access to have access to healthcare.

Since we are plotting poverty and lack of healthcare, we see a positive linear correlation.

Mississippi and New Mexico in particular stand out for both high levels of poverty and lack of healthcare, while states such as Massachusetts, Maryland, and Connecticut have the lowest levels of poverty and highest access to healthcare. Texas in particular stands out for the high percentage of its population that does not have access to healthcare though poverty levels are not as bad.
